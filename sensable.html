QUESTION

can we create dynamic & inclusive experiences by targeting multiple senses as interaction points?

Often, the default in interaction design is to focus on visual interactions, but this leaves a lot to be desired when people can't interact with those experiences because of an impairment. This approach is further limited by a single experience for all users. To combat this, I completed an exploration of how to design sensory interactions and utilize artificial intelligence to create personalized, accessible experiences. To give this exploration real-life context, I created journeys for users who want to learn about sustainable fashion, an industry with a reputation for a lack of diversity.

NEED SPACE

inclusive educational experiences drive engagement

As the sustainable fashion movement has migrated from the fringes to mainstream markets, customers are searching for reliable resources to educate themselves. However, one criticism of the sustainable fashion industry is its lack of inclusivity. A lot of these brands don't use diverse models in terms of size, race, age or ability, and many of their price points are out of the budget of many shoppers. This leads to a culture of shaming those who can't afford to shop "sustainably."

SENSORY AUDIT

eliminate pain points by diversifying interactions

When comparing interactive experiences used daily, most heavily rely on one or two senses. Amazon's Alexa uses sound. Most desktop web experiences use sight. By developing multiple ways to interact and varying the types of interactions, a more inclusive and dynamic experience emerges. Finally, employing AI shifts the pressure from the user to define the terms of the experience and instead, puts that burden on the AI, creating simplified, yet robust interactions.

MOTIVATION

access to resources encourages commitment

Creating a sustainable fashion space is too important to alienate people who want to help. That's where Sensable comes in. It casts a wider net by arming shoppers with reliable facts and resources that help them make more ethical and sustainable choices regardless of economic status. It further emphasizes inclusivity with its wide range of interaction capabilities and expansive, personalized experiences through AI.

COMPETITIVE AUDIT

verify learning through quizzes

Learning does not end with problem awareness. After completing a competitive audit of learning apps including Duolingo, I added a "quiz" function to the experience so people could see their knowledge improving as they use Sensable. The commitment to creating multiple interaction methods extended here to keep the quiz taking fun, not academic.

PERSONA #1

alexandra, 33

GOALS Uncover the privilege and inclusivity problem prevalent in sustainable fashion

Alexandra is starting her own sustainable fashion line focusing on inclusive sizing. She's an expert on sustainable fashion, but finds the industry almost exclusively caters to women of a certain size, shape and skin color. She wants to skip the facts she already knows, and learn about the privilege problem.

PERSONA #2

paulina, 65, visually impaired

GOALS Learn about brands and tactics that help her make more informed, ethical purchases

A conversation with Paulina's granddaughter sparked her interest in sustainable fashion. She has a median level of knowledge, but wants to find out more about the industry's social and economic impact. She's self-motivated, but often finds herself discouraged with mobile experiences of her visually impairment.

PERSONA #3

sydney, 24, hearing impaired

GOALS Discover the best way to dispose of old clothes and learn about the impact of waste in fashion

Sydney is a model who accumulates a lot of clothing when she's doing photo shoots. She's a beginner in learning about sustainable fashion, so she wants to learn about the best practices when it comes to recycling, selling and reusing clothing. She's a digital native, but her hearing impairment limits her access to experiences.

PAPER WIREFRAMES

creating useful wireframes for a dynamic experience

When desiging for AI, the experience can change so much so quickly with each user, so typical wireframes would be unnecessarily time consuming. I rapidly prototyped the information architecture on index cards to help guide the hi-fidelity experience screens.

VISUAL IDENTITY

developing a visual identity for multiple meanings

Sensable's identity is built on the idea of contrasts, so visual elements like the organic edges of the torn paper and the earthy color palette contrast with the monospaced ultra-technical typography. The logo takes the same approach with the soft handlettered "S" whose shape suggests a needle and thread creating a stitch. This stitch can also be interpreted as a break, calling to Sensable's emphasis on breaking the fashion industry's practices. Finally, the "S" evokes the visual of an infinity sign to emphasize the important lasting effects of shopping sustainably.

NEXT STEPS

finding larger testing groups

The ideation phase of this project took the most time as I experimented with different potential ways to interact with this product. I want to complete a series of user tests with participants who live with a variety of impairments and who feel under-represented in interactive experiences.
